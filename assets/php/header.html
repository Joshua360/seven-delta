Kimathi Gitachuri -Principal Consultant

Kimathi Githachuri is a Digital Financial Services expert, with over ten years’ experience in mass markets in Africa and Asia; blending practitioner experience in three pioneering mobile money deployments in Kenya and Uganda and, as a Management Consultant, supporting multiple regulators, financial institutions and mobile network operators across the world.
He was part of the team that helped roll out Safaricom’s M-PESA mobile money service in Kenya. Kimathi’s latest work includes Opportunity International in India, Ghana and Malawi, Facebook’s global retail project (for Tanzania, DRC, Senegal, Pakistan and Bangladesh), National Shared Agency Banking Infrastructure Service for Rwanda, The National Payments Systems Project for Ghana, Equity Bank Agency Banking and MTN Mobile Money in Uganda, Bank Mandiri agency banking deployment support in Indonesia.
He is also currently lead technical specialist (Anglophone) in a five-year MasterCard Foundation funded project covering six countries, and managed under the World Savings & Retail Banking Institute (WSBI) Prior to joining Seven-Delta Consulting, Kimathi was involved in the launch, and served since 2013 to 2016, as the Principal Consultant and first Head of The Helix Institute of Digital Finance, a collaborative effort by MicroSave Consulting, supported by The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, United Nations Capital Development Fund (UNCDF), International Finance Corporation (IFC) and the Financial Sector Deepening - Africa (FSD-A).
Kimathi previously worked for Warid Telecom Uganda where, as Head of Warid Pesa, he led the deployment of the mobile money service that was voted joint global winners with Barclays Bank Plc. for ‘Best New Mobile Money Service 2012’, and listed as GSMA Sprinter (2013). He also played a leading role in the product design and commercial roll-out of a national mobile-based tax-payment system in partnership with Uganda Revenue Authority (URA), Orient Bank Ltd and Equity Bank Uganda Ltd, a first in Sub-Sahara Africa. Kimathi leads the Digital Financial Inclusion Practice at Seven-Delta. He is an MBA graduate from The University of Nairobi, and is currently pursuing a PhD degree in Business Administration from the same university.
